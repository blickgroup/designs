Design notes — Stage 2: Planning (v4 — DoD as close-gate, not dashboard)

v4 change from v3

Definition of Done is out of the daily view. v3 put a 4-row DoD card in both the main column and the side rail — it dominated a page users will live in for weeks. DoD only matters when closing. v4 demotes it to a small side-rail pill (Close-out · 0/4) and moves the real work into a close-gate modal triggered by "Mark complete". See the modal overlaid in this mockup.

Research basis — how established tools handle it

Surveyed Monday, Linear, Asana, Jira, ClickUp, Basecamp, Notion. Three things emerged:

  1. No tool puts DoD in the header or dominant panel day-to-day. Universally a quiet sidebar/panel with a progress count, if visible at all.
  2. Close-time surfacing is a modal or transition screen. Jira's transition-screen validator is the gold standard — hit Done, a modal lists unmet criteria and blocks/soft-gates the move.
  3. Soft gate with audit trail > hard block. ClickUp's "Incomplete Warning" framing ("these items are still open — confirm or add a reason") matches our skip-with-reason model better than Jira's hard validator. Reasons get recorded and surface on the Done view for the retro.

Worst offender: Basecamp (no gate at all, one-click archive). Lesson: don't under-gate either — a silent archive invites sliding projects to Done with loose ends.

How v4 surfaces DoD

  • Day-to-day: single side-rail pill "Close-out · 0/4" with a chevron. Collapsed by default. Clicking expands to show the 4 template rows inline, read-only. No ticking happens here.
  • When closing: owner clicks Mark complete (top-right action). A modal opens listing every DoD item with two actions per row: ✓ Done or ↷ Skip with reason. Skip reveals a text field. All rows must have one or the other before submit. Custom project-specific items appear below template items.
  • After submit: project moves to Done stage. Skipped items with their reasons are recorded and appear on the Done view (stage 4) as part of the project's closing record — visible at cycle retro.

Main column focus

Updates (activity feed), follow-ups, and documentation. That's the daily surface. The brief is at the top because it answers "why are we doing this", and setup is a one-line dismissible banner. Nothing else competes for attention.

Other carried-over v3 decisions

  • Owner assigned at allocation (Tim Walters), non-admins see read-only with "Reassign · admin only" chip.
  • "Tasks" renamed to "Follow-ups" hub-wide.
  • No Related Vault Notes at planning stage — moves to Execution/Done when there's actual context to surface.

Open questions for Shaun

  • At close: hard gate (can't submit until every row is either ticked or has a reason) or soft gate (can submit, reasons optional but surfaced)? v4 mocks the hard-gate-with-skip-as-escape valve approach.
  • Who sees the Mark-complete button — owner only, or also admins?
  • Should skipped-with-reason items trigger any admin review step, or is the reason on record enough?
  • Blickopedia — real system name or placeholder for "internal knowledge base"?
